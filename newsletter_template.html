The Great Post-Holiday Reset: Reclaiming Your Space

The tree is down, the glitter is finally swept away, and your home might feel a little... quiet. But instead of seeing your space as "empty," try viewing it as a fresh canvas. The weeks following the holidays are the perfect time to breathe new life into your home with a few intentional updates.

1. The "Winter Un-Decorate"

Before you rush to put your everyday decor back in place, pause. Let your surfaces stay clear for a few days. Allow the architectural details of your room—the mantle, the bookshelves, the coffee table—to breathe. This "visual silence" helps you see your space with fresh eyes and decide what you really love enough to bring back.

2. Warmth Without the Kitsch

You don't need holiday decor to make a room feel cozy. Swap out the festive plaids for sophisticated winter textures. Think rich velvet pillows in deep navy or forest green, chunky knit throws in cream or oatmeal, and faux fur accents. These layers add physical and visual warmth that lasts until spring.

3. Bring Life Back to the Corner

That empty spot where the Christmas tree stood can feel glaringly bare. Turn that void into a focal point! It’s the perfect location for a statement reading chair, a large basket with extra blankets, or a tall indoor plant like a Fiddle Leaf Fig or Olive Tree to bring fresh energy and oxygen into your home.

2025 Design Resolutions: Intentional Living

This year, let's move beyond fleeting trends and focus on creating a home that supports your life. Here are three resolutions to guide your design choices in 2025.

Resolution #1: "I will design for how I actually live."

Stop saving the "good room" for guests who visit twice a year. In 2025, we’re reclaiming every square foot. Choose performance fabrics that can handle coffee spills and muddy paws. Opt for deep, comfortable sectionals that fit the whole family for movie night. Your home should be your sanctuary, not a museum.

Resolution #2: "I will find my signature style."

It’s easy to get lost in the endless scroll of Pinterest and Instagram. This year, commit to finding your look. Not sure if you're "Modern Farmhouse," "Coastal Cool," or "Classic Traditional"? Our design team is here to help you translate your likes into a cohesive look that flows from room to room.

Resolution #3: "I will finish what I started."

We all have that one room. The guest room that’s currently a storage unit. The dining room with the mismatched chairs. The home office that’s just a table in the corner. Let 2025 be the year of completion. Pick one space and commit to seeing it through—from the area rug up to the lighting fixtures. You’ll be amazed at how much lighter you feel when your home feels complete.
